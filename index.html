Grig Bilham

Technologist, Electronic Musician, Nonprofit Strategist

Grig Bilham is a technologist, electronic musician, and nonprofit strategist who has spent more than 25 years designing open systems that keep artists in charge of their work. He helped pioneer equitable digital music in 2004 by co‑founding Addictech, an underground electronic‑music store that paid independent producers on fair splits and shipped DRM‑free catalogues long before such terms were common.

After exiting Addictech in 2018, Grig moved fluidly between consultancy and early‑stage co‑founder roles, delivering over sixty web, VR, and AI products for clients such as Disney‑ABC, Warner Music, Autodesk, and a swarm of seed‑stage start‑ups. His current umbrella vehicle, Distributed Creatives, operates Forever Sites, Musely.social, and the Local Artist Network—projects aimed at preserving creative works for centuries while ensuring that 100% of revenue flows back to their makers.

On the standards front, Grig co‑chairs the Digital Asset Management Working Group within the Metaverse Standards Forum, where he is helping draft open metadata and provenance formats for 3‑D and Web media. He also maintains FractalMesh, a modular social‑network stack, and Thinkr, an AI‑orchestration layer that automates research and code for distributed teams.

Performing internationally under the alias Neuronaut, he blends modular synthesis with large‑scale visuals to advocate for ethical AI and resilient creator economies. Across dozens of community‑owned ventures on five continents, his principle remains constant:

Tools should outlast platforms—and creators should out‑earn intermediaries.
